# Food Calendar Rules

Starting March 28, 2026, create a daily calendar for planned food introductions until September 19, 2026.

## Rules

- No brand new foods on consecutive days.  
  - Example: if egg was never introduced and broccoli was never introduced, they cannot be introduced immediately after each other.
- At least 3 days between allergenic foods on the calendar for the first time.
- No combinations.  
  - Example: pancakes, which utilize egg and wheat and dairy, cannot be used until all ingredients have been introduced separately.
- Allergens must be present 1–2x each week after their initial introduction.
- Each day must have at least one food item.
- Non-allergenic foods do not need to be repeated in any fixed quantity, but they can be repeated.
- Use as many foods on the provided list as possible, while prioritizing the earlier rules.
- Recipes combining the above foods can be utilized as combination foods.
- Look up these recipes independently.
- Added sodium, sugar, and hot spices are not allowed in any recipes.
- Do not add combination foods to the calendar until May 1.
